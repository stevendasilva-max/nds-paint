## **Content Writing Principles**

* Write so clearly a 7th grader can understand it — dumb it down  
* Aim for slightly more word count than the current \#1 ranking page  
* **Long-tail keywords:** The searcher knows exactly what they want and is far more likely to purchase  
* **Branded search** makes ranking easier and is a clear signal your marketing is working  
* Use numbered lists and bullets — readers love them and they keep people engaged  
* One image per 200 words of content

---

## **Keyword Usage**

* **Keyword density:** Aim slightly higher than your top competitor (1–2 extra uses)  
* Place your keyword in:  
  * Title tag  
  * Meta description  
  * H1 tag  
  * At least one H2 tag  
  * Naturally throughout the body

---

## **Title Tags**

* Make them slightly click-baity  
  * **Bad:** "NASA is looking for participants for their bed rest studies"  
  * **Good:** "NASA Will Pay You $18,000 to Stay in Bed for 70 Straight Days"  
* Approximately 60 characters, 8–15 words  
* Template: `[Keyword] | [Benefit/Dream Outcome] | [Brand Name]`  
* Must be unique across your entire site

---

## **Meta Descriptions**

* 80–156 characters  
* Include primary keyword near the beginning  
* Google doesn't directly factor them into rankings — don't stress over them

---

## **URLs**

* Include the relevant keyword (except the homepage, which stays generic)  
* Don't keyword stuff — use it once  
* Use subfolders appropriately: `/blog`, `/services`, `/products`, `/press`, `/careers`

---

## **Heading Tags**

| Tag | Usage |
| ----- | ----- |
| H1 | Only one per page; under 60 characters; keyword at the beginning |
| H2 | At least 2 per page; subtopics that support the H1; keyword not required at start |
| H3 | Supporting details; only use when it makes sense for the reader |
| H4–H6 | If you need these, the topic is too deep — split it into separate pages |

* Flow logically: H1 → H2 → H3 (you can return from H3 back up to H2 as needed)  
* When headings align with "People Also Ask" and "People Also Search," you can earn **rich snippets** which improve click-through rate (CTR)

---

## **Internal Links**

* 3–5 internal links per page minimum  
* At least one must point to a product or service (money) page  
* Use **descriptive anchor text** (1–5 words): e.g., *"how to choose running shoes"* not *"read more"*  
* Add alt text to all images  
* Use breadcrumbs to quietly boost site architecture  
* **Navigational links (header):** Services, Products, About, Contact, Blog  
* **Footer links:** FAQ, policies, support pages, location-specific content  
* **Site structure:** Home page → service pages → contact page → content hub (blog)  
* Post blog content several times per week, each written with keyword strategy  
* When a new page is published, link to and from it at least 3 times (no duplicate anchor text)  
* All pages should be reachable within **3 clicks** from the home page  
* Keep the latest 5–10 blog posts visible on the home page  
* Link to all blog categories from the home page

---

## **External Links**

* Shows Google your content is well-researched and based on facts, not opinions  
* At least one external link per page, especially when citing facts, stats, or tools  
* Only link to reputable, relevant sources  
* Do **not** link to competitors or duplicate content  
* Do **not** use your target keyword as anchor text for external links (you'd be helping them rank)  
* Use anchor text like *"source"* or *"according to \[XYZ\]"* instead

---

## **Images**

* **File formats:** PNG for logos and icons; WebP for photos (JPG is the next best option)  
* Keep each image under **250KB**  
* Photos of your team, office, and happy clients build trust  
* Use infographics  
* **Alt text:** Describe what the image actually shows — don't keyword stuff  
  * Good example: *"illustration of a stack of blueberry pancakes in a kitchen"*

---

## **Video**

* Keeps people on your page longer, which is a positive SEO signal  
* Host on YouTube, Vimeo, or Loom — **do not self-host** (it will slow your site)  
* Google indexes **one video per page**  
* Embedding others' videos helps them rank, so use your own when possible  
* Record audio calls or discussions → transcribe → use as a blog post starting point  
* Enhance posts with heading tags, images, and a video for a stronger user experience  
* Edit video content with Canva or CapCut

---

## **Backlinks**

* Domain authority \= trust score  
* Quality and **variety** of referring domains matters more than total backlink count  
* Focus on diversity, not just volume  
* Reciprocal links dilute value — triangle links are better  
* Negative SEO via bad links doesn't work anymore  
* **Spam score:** 1% is great; 30% or higher is a problem  
* Do **not** use the disavow tool  
* **How many do you need?** Check the referring domains of top-ranking competitors and aim to match or exceed them  
* Reach out to link owners and offer more valuable or more up-to-date content to earn or replace their links  
* Post genuinely helpful content consistently  
* If you're in a niche where ads aren't allowed, create a micro-site one-pager adjacent to the main site in an area where ads are permitted  
* When someone reaches out requesting a backlink: make them link to you first, then give them what they wanted

## **Local SEO**

* **Directories:** Yelp, Yellow Pages, 411, Foursquare — get reviews on these too, primarily Yelp  
* **NAP consistency:** Name, Address, and Phone number must be 100% identical everywhere  
* Get involved in networking events, community initiatives, charity drives, and sponsorships  
* Match or exceed top 3–5 local competitors' Chamber of Commerce links  
* **At networking events:** Don't talk about yourself. Don't try to sell anything.

## **Technical SEO**

* **WordPress:** Use WP Rocket plugin; avoid Bluehost — try CrocWeb for speed  
* **Shopify:** Use a Fiverr gig to optimize site speed  
* **Mobile first** — Google and users both prefer it  
* Replace slow carousels with one clear image and an attention-grabbing headline

### **Core Web Vitals**

| Metric | Target |
| ----- | ----- |
| LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) | Under 2.5 seconds |
| INP (Interaction to Next Paint) | Under 200 ms |
| CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) | No layout shifts |

## **Duplicate Content**

When duplicate content exists, choose one of the following resolutions:

1. Merge content and 301 redirect  
2. 301 redirect only  
3. Update the content  
4. Add a canonical tag *(common on Shopify product pages with small variations)*

After publishing a page, confirm that the word count matches your brief.

---

## **Orphan Pages**

* Pages that are totally disconnected from the rest of the site  
* Useful for funnel and landing pages intentionally  
* Avoid unintentional orphan pages by linking to and from every new page at least 3 times on publish

## **Keyword Research Process**

1. Always start with keyword research before writing anything  
2. Analyze competitor websites and specific pages  
3. Use SEMRush Keyword Gap tool  
4. Paste competitor URLs into AI and ask: *"What keywords is this page optimized for?"*  
5. Check "People Also Ask" for blog post ideas  
6. Use Answer the Public for question-based keyword ideas  
7. Pull from questions you receive from real leads and customers  
8. **Start with low-competition long-tail keywords** (e.g., *"local plumbing companies in Detroit"* before *"plumber Detroit"*)  
9. Low-volume strategy: 2 blogs per week targeting 10–100 monthly searches equals roughly 60,000 searches per year — and with improved topical authority, you'll earn the right to rank for more competitive terms over time

## **Content Writing & Optimization Process**

### **Step 1 — Build a Mini Content Roadmap**

Create a spreadsheet using: SEO Chrome Extension, a keyword tool, Content Analyzer, and Google PageSpeed Insights. For the top 3–5 competing pages, track:

* Keyword density  
* Word count  
* Where and how often they use the keyword  
* Page speed  
* Backlinks and referring domains

### **Step 2 — Build Your Content Skeleton**

1. Open the top 3–5 competitor pages  
2. Copy their headings  
3. Remove irrelevant ones (e.g., "Related Posts")  
4. Insert relevant secondary keywords as H2s or H3s where they make sense  
5. Reword the headings slightly — this is your content skeleton

### **Step 3 — Draft the Content**

1. Copy content from top 3–5 competitors  
2. Have AI summarize the content  
3. Paste the summary into your skeleton and outline  
4. Prompt AI: *"Write an article based on this outline"*  
5. Add your own experience, expertise, and personal stories  
6. Ensure H1 has the target keyword at the front  
7. Ensure one or more H2s use the keyword  
8. Fact-check everything  
9. Add images, videos, internal links, and external sources  
10. Check your roadmap: if competitors use the keyword 7 times, use it 8 times

## **Product Pages**

* Use descriptive keyword-based names (e.g., *"mid-century modern sofa"*) not internal product names like "Florpavia"  
* Treat product pages like blog posts — add internal links to related products and helpful blog content  
* **Page layout from top to bottom:** product photos → product name with keyword → star rating → price → Add to Cart button → everything else below the fold

---

## **Category Pages**

* Break broad categories into specific ones: *"stovetop espresso makers"* instead of *"coffee machines"*  
* More specific pages \= more keywords targeted \= more rankings

---

## **Service Pages**

Each service gets its own dedicated page. The target keyword must appear in:

* H1  
* At least one H2  
* The URL  
* The title tag  
* The meta description  
* Sprinkled naturally throughout the body content  
* Internal links on the page
